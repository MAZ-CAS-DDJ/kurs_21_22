Bipartisan experts and military\nleaders have said that our security would be best served by having defense\nspending share in the sacrifice. The president\'s bipartisan debt commission\nplan actually foresaw defense cuts twice as large as the president\'s plan -\na reminder that there\'s a great deal to work with as we tend to the economic\nfoundations of our strength."\n\n\n\nAs part of the changes to defense spending, the president\'s speech laid down\na plan to "conduct a fundamental review of America\'s missions, capabilities\nand our role in a changing world."\n\n\n\nNSN Senior Adviser Maj. Gen. (ret.) Paul Eaton says such a review is\nessential to an effective national security strategy. "Every commander has\nto at once prioritize, declare the main effort and manage risk. The command\nwill never be everything to everyone," Eaton said. "In today\'s constrained\neconomic environment, the president\'s review should focus on how America can\nlead its allies and the world via a whole-of-government approach --\nemploying cost-effective tools such as diplomacy and development when\npossible and reverting to the military only when necessary."\n\n\n\n###\n\n\n\n*Background*\n\n\n\n\xb7         "Total defense spending in real terms is now higher than at any\ntime since the end of World War\nII<http://r20.rs6.net/tn.jsp?llr=olnitxbab&et=1105162544883&s=15897&e=001Rx_sKffgjm0JpmtLlwQhLCYd3YfN-u_AK5j9Fe0sHvWiKdOQ0FW6bHGxG1-2NFo1Ywf6vpVjG8nauEnNn6tolfAd8ERcCjwE1w75zOk8L0U9xOsNaWZb4xWmpOyeodHCYw8JsFudDlbB8snpagmnAsduupCdQX3fZKe2LEDGibkw3Jw2wqYrMg==>,\nmore than throughout the entire Cold War and even 10 percent higher than the\npeak of the Reagan defense buildup.
