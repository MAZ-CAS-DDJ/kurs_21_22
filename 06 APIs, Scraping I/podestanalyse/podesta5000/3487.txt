Central banks have responded with innovative policies to address these challenges. At the same time, fiscal authorities in many countries are tightly constrained. As a result, either explicitly or implicitly, many governments are ceding marcoeconomic policy authority to their central banks. Is this sustainable in the long run? With large cross border capital flows adding complexity, should central bankers actively coordinate their policies? Is this possible without compromising national objectives and central bank independence?
